  1. Feist Publications, Inc. v. Rural Telephone Service Co. (1991):


    2. Outcome: Feist Publications won. The Supreme Court ruled in favor of Feist, establishing the standard that factual compilations require a minimal level of creativity for copyright protection.

  2. Sony Corp. of America v. Universal City Studios, Inc. (1984):


    3. Outcome: Sony won. The Supreme Court held that the sale of videocassette recorders (VCRs) for home recording did not constitute contributory copyright infringement.

  3. Campbell v. Acuff-Rose Music, Inc. (1994):


    4. Outcome: 2 Live Crew (Campbell) won. The Supreme Court ruled that 2 Live Crew's parody of Roy Orbison's song "Pretty Woman" was a transformative use and constituted fair use.

  4. Eldred v. Ashcroft (2003):


  5. Outcome: The U.S. Congress (Ashcroft) won. The Supreme Court upheld the constitutionality of the Copyright Term Extension Act, extending copyright terms by 20 years.

  6. Golan v. Holder (2012):


    7. Outcome: The U.S. Congress (Holder) won. The Supreme Court ruled that removing certain works from the public domain under the Uruguay Round Agreements Act was constitutional.

  7. Kirtsaeng v. John Wiley & Sons, Inc. (2013):


    8. Outcome: Kirtsaeng won. The Supreme Court held that the first-sale doctrine applies to copies of a copyrighted work lawfully made abroad, allowing the resale of such copies in the U.S.

  8. Google LLC v. Oracle America, Inc. (2021):

  9. Outcome: Google won. The Supreme Court ruled that Google's use of certain elements of Oracle's Java application programming interface (API) constituted fair use.
